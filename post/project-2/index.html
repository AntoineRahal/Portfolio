Link to Dashboard

The economic crisis had a very profound impact on Lebanese society as a whole. It negatively affected the productivity and mental health of many and ignited the desire for emigration in people who had never considered it previously. In addition, as was shown in the survey the majority of the respondents are pessimistic about the future in Lebanon. However, a large number of respondents never considered leaving Lebanon before the economic crisis and a large number of them would stay if the situation improved. When asked about which aspect of the economic collapse if solved would make them stay in Lebanon, the majority of the respondents agreed that the devaluation of the currency is their biggest concern. To help alleviate the impact of the crisis on Lebanese society two solutions were proposed.

  • First, is the establishment of an independent currency board to stabilize inflation, countries in difficult economic conditions which established independent currency boards like Estonia in 1992, Lithuania in 1994, and Bulgaria in 1997 have succeeded in maintaining inflation rates low and stable for 20 years.
  • Second, the encouragement of patents could contribute to the increase in the country’s GDP. Lebanon’s Human Capital Index performs relatively close to the two economic powerhouses of the region which are Saudi Arabia and the UAE. In order to take advantage of that fact, the Lebanese youth should work to obtain and commercialize patents in the digital service space which will diminish inflation, brain drain, and unemployment.

Both these solutions are not the only ones available for Lebanon and they have some conditions, for example, the use of patents requires a robust infrastructure, a perpetual availability of affordable and sustainable electricity, and a fast and stable internet connection. However, both these solutions highlight the fact that despite the dire economic situation, there is still possibility for sustainable growth.
